K-12 Scope & Sequence

Home > Academics > K-12 Scope & Sequence

The curriculum followed at Stonehaven is demanding of even the most gifted students so as to maximize their God-given gifts and talents.

The work is challenging... but so is the world our young people will inevitably enter one day. Students will need to be able to spot and then handle the falsehoods and equivocations rampant in the secular world which insidiously draws in the gullible and unprepared. It is the task of Stonehaven to sharpen the minds of its students, to teach them to think logically, methodically, deeply, and critically. Students will be taught to "question all things."

Kindergarten

  • Bible - Select stories & character studies in Old and New Testaments
  • Reading & Language Arts - Abeka Phonics , beginning primers, Zaner-Bloser Handwriting
  • History & Geography - Brief introduction to American figures, map skills, holidays
  • Mathematics - Saxon Math K and supplements
  • Science - Studies in senses, seasons, nutrition, plants, and animals
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

First Grade

  • Bible - Select stories in Old Testament
  • Reading & Language Arts - Abeka Phonics 1, Shurley Grammar 1, Grammar of Spelling 1, Zaner-Bloser Handwriting
  • History & Geography - SOTW 1 Ancient Civilizations, Greece and Rome, (Creation - 400 AD)
  • Mathematics - Saxon Math 1 and supplements
  • Science - Beginning activities & instruction emphasizing observation & discovery Science 1 BJUP
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Second Grade

  • Bible - Select stories in New Testament
  • Reading & Language Arts - Shurley Grammar 2, Grammar of Spelling 2, Zaner-Bloser Cursive
  • History & Geography - SOTW 2 Medieval, Renaissance, Reformation (400 - 1600 AD)
  • Mathematics - Saxon Math 2 and supplements
  • Science - Exploring Creation with Astronomy
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Third Grade

  • Bible - God's Great Covenant : Old Testament 1 (Genesis - Ruth)
  • Reading & Language Arts - Shurley Grammar 3, Grammar of Spelling 3
  • History & Geography - SOTW 3 Early Modern Era (1600 - 1850 AD)
  • Mathematics - Saxon Math 3 and supplements
  • Science - Exploring Creation with Zoology 1 Birds & Insects
  • Latin - Coversational vocabulary, verbal comprehension, intro. to Roman culture; Minimus - Starting Out Cambridge Latin
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Fourth Grade

  • Bible - God's Great Covenant: Old Testament 2 (Samuel - Malachi)
  • Reading & Language Arts - Rod & Staff English 4, Grammar of Spelling 4, Imitation in Writing/Classical Conversations for Fables and Fairytales
  • History & Geography - SOTW 4 Modern Era (1850 - present)
  • Mathematics - Saxon Intermediate Math 4
  • Science - Exploring Creation with Botany
  • Latin - Vocabulary, Grammar, Verbal & Reading Comprehension; Cambridge Latin Course & Lingua Latina
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Fifth Grade

  • Bible - Study of the Gospels
  • Reading & Language Arts - Rod & Staff Engish 5, Grammar of Spelling 5 , Grammar of Poetry, Imitation in Writing Greek Myths
  • History & Geography - SOTW 1 Ancient Civilizations , Greece and Rome, (Creation - 400 AD)
  • Mathematics - Saxon Intermediate Math 5
  • Science - Exploring Creation with Zoology 2 & 3 : Swimming Creatures and Land Animals; beginning Human Anatomy and Physiology
  • Latin - Vocabulary, Grammar, Verbal & Reading Comprehension; Cambridge Latin Course & Lingua Latina
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Sixth Grade

  • Bible - Acts and Paul's Epistles
  • Reading & Language Arts - Classical Medieval Literature, Vocab Workshop
  • History & Geography - Streams of Civilization Book 1 , Medieval, Renaissance, Reformation (400 - 1600 AD)
  • Mathematics - Sadlier Oxford - Progress in Math
  • Science - Exploring Creation with Human Anatomy and Physiology
  • Latin - Vocabulary, Grammar, Verbal & Reading Comprehension, Oral & Written Composition; Lingua Latina 1 - 7
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Seventh Grade

  • Bible - Old Testament I: Genesis - 2 Kings
  • Literature - Early Modern Literature (Essential Texts: Tartuffe, Much Ado About Nothing, Frankenstein, The Adventures of Tom Sawyer )
  • History - Medieval / Early Modern History ( Streams of Civilization, Vol. II , Sources of the Western Tradition , Marvin Perry)
  • Mathematics - Pre-Algebra ( Sadlier Oxford, Fundamentals of Algebra )
  • Science - Earth Science ( Novare Science )
  • Logic/Rhetoric - Formal Logic ( Traditional Logic: Introduction to Formal Logic , Martin Cothran)
  • Foreign Language - Latin ( Lingua Latina per se Illustrata , Hans Orberg)
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Eighth Grade

  • Bible - Old Testament II: 1 Chronicles-Malachi
  • Literature - Modern Literature (Essential Texts: Fahrenheit 451, A Tale of Two Cities, Twelve Years a Slave, Romeo and Juliet, To Kill a Mockingbird )
  • History - Modern History ( A Concise History of the United States , Robert Remini, The Boisterous Sea of Liberty, Mintz and Davis)
  • Mathematics - Algebra I (Harold Jacobs, Elementary Algebra )
  • Science - Physical Science ( Novare Science )
  • Logic/Rhetoric - Informal Logic ( The Art of Argument: An Introduction to the Informal Fallacies , Aaron Larsen and Joelle Hodge with Chris Perrin)
  • Foreign Language - Latin I ( Lingua Latina per se Illustrata , Hans Orberg) or Spanish I ( The Comprehensible Classroom , SOMOS Spanish Edition)
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Ninth Grade

  • Bible - New Testament Survey (ESV Bible)
  • Literature - Ancient Literature
  • History - Ancient History
  • Mathematics - Geometry ( Harold Jacobs , Geometry, Euclid's Elements )
  • Science - General Biology ( Novare Science )
  • Logic/Rhetoric - Dialectic ( Traditional Logic II , Martin Cothran and Scott Piland)
  • Foreign Language - Latin II ( Lingua Latina per se Illustrata , Hans Orberg) or Spanish II ( The Comprehensible Classroom , SOMOS Spanish Edition)
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Tenth Grade

  • Bible - Church History ( Eusebius Church History )
  • Literature - Medieval / Early Modern Literature
  • History - Medieval / Early Modern History
  • Mathematics - Algebra II & Trigonometry (Paul Foerster, Algebra II & Trigonometry: Functions and Applications )
  • Science - General Chemistry ( Novare Science )
  • Logic/Rhetoric - Rhetoric I: Debate ( Rhetoric Alive , Book I, Alyssan Barnes)
  • Foreign Language - Latin III ( Lingua Latina per se Illustrata , Hans Orberg) or Spanish III ( The Comprehensible Classroom , SOMOS Spanish Edition)
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Eleventh Grade

  • Bible - Biblical Worldview/Ethics/Apologetics (James N. Anderson Why Should I Believe Christianity? )
  • Literature - American Literature
  • History - American History
  • Mathematics - Pre-Calculus
  • Science - Introductory Physics ( Novare Science )
  • Logic/Rhetoric - Rhetoric II: Declamation ( Declamation , Classical Academic Press)
  • Foreign Language - Latin IV (Original Latin Texts, Latin Novellas) or Spanish IV ( The Comprehensible Classroom , SOMOS Spanish Edition)
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Twelfth Grade

  • Bible - Redemptive History
  • Literature - British Literature
  • History - Modern History
  • Mathematics - Calculus / Statistics
  • Science - Advanced Science
  • Logic/Rhetoric - Rhetoric Capstone ( Six Great Ideas , Mortimer Adler)
  • Foreign Language - Latin V (Original Latin Texts) or Spanish V (Spanish Short stories and novels)
  • Cocurriculars - Music, Art, & Physical Education

Grammar (Pre-Polly)

Grades: K-2
Ages: 4-8

Characteristics

  • Obviously excited about learning
  • Enjoys games, stories, songs, projects
  • Short attention span
  • Wants to touch, taste, feel, smell, see
  • Imaginative, creative

Grammar (Poll-Parrot)

Grades: 3-6
Ages: 9-11

Characteristics

  • Excited about new, interesting facts
  • Likes to explain, figure out, talk
  • Wants to relate own experiences to topic, or just to tell a story
  • Likes collections, organizing items
  • Likes chants, clever, repetitious word sounds
  • Easily memorizes
  • Can assimilate another language well

Logic (Pert)

Grades: 7-9
Ages: 12-14

Characteristics

  • Still excitable, but needs challenges
  • Judges, critiques, debates, critical
  • Likes to organize items, others
  • Shows off knowledge
  • Wants to know "behind the scenes" facts
  • Curious about Why? for most things
  • Thinks, acts as though more knowledgeable than adults

Rhetoric (Poetic)

Grades: 10-12
Ages: 15-18

Characteristics

  • Concerned with present events, especially in own life
  • Interested in justice, fairness
  • Moving toward special interests, topics
  • Can take on responsibility, independent work
  • Can do synthesis
  • Desires to express feelings, own ideas
  • Generally idealistic

"They do not know what the words mean; they do not know how to ward them off or blunt their edge or fling them back; they are a prey to words in their emotions instead of being the masters of them in their intellects." - Dorothy Sayers
