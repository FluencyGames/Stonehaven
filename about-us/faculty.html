Grammar School

Laura Keesee

Kindergarten

Laura Keesee earned a B.S. in Psychology from King College in Bristol, Tennessee, a Master of Education in Community Counseling from Georgia State University, and a Certificate of Completion from Psychological Studies Institute (now Richmont Graduate University) in Atlanta. In 2019, she received training in the Orton-Gillingham approach, participating in workshops through The Dyslexia Resource, which works in partnership with The Schenck School. This training enriched her knowledge of language and strengthened her passion and ability to teach phonics and reading. Thanks to Laura, many Stonehaven staff as well as students now enjoy counting syllables using “whale talk” and finding the accented syllable by “calling the dog”. Laura is excited to begin her 23rd year teaching Kindergarten, 16 of which have been at Stonehaven, where she is thankful for the privilege of partnering with parents in the education, nurture, and godly training of their children. In addition to teaching at Stonehaven, Laura has enjoyed opportunities to teach English and lead Bible studies in Japan, India, and Albania. She and her husband Scott have been married for 34 years and are the parents of three adult children. Scott and Laura are active members at Midway Presbyterian Church (PCA). When not teaching or spending time with her family, she enjoys knitting, crocheting, and reading historical fiction, mysteries, and the works of great theologians.

Grace Potter

First Grade

Grace Potter graduated from Covenant College with a BA in Biblical & Theological studies and a minor in education. After graduation, she served for 2 years as an intern with Reformed University Fellowship (RUF) at Kennesaw State University. Grace loves children and is passionate about helping them learn and grow in all areas of life. She was greatly impacted by the Christian education she received throughout her life and is excited for the opportunity to invest in the lives of children through teaching. Her desire is to help cultivate in the next generation a love for God and His Word, and a desire to love and serve others. Grace is a member of Christ Community Church in Kennesaw, GA. She enjoys a variety of sports and outdoor activities, her favorites being basketball and mountain biking.

Erin Denny

Second Grade

Erin Denny graduated from College of Charleston with a BS in Business Administration and Marketing. She has spent the last 18 years investing in her family’s small business with her husband, Mike. However, her greatest passion has been raising her family, which led her to classically homeschool their three children for 7 years. While homeschooling, she taught on weekly community days at Classical Conversations Smyrna until God led her family to Stonehaven. Stonehaven has been the greatest gift to her family the past three years. She joined the Stonehaven staff part-time in 2020-2021 as the Blended Learning Specialist and is incredibly humbled to teach second grade this year. She is most excited to partner with parents to instill in her students a deep joy of learning, disciple their hearts towards the Lord, and encourage their God-given gifts so they will grow to make God known in the world. Some of her favorite things are date nights with her husband, hiking with her family, cooking, and reading good stories with her children, preferably with a cup of hot tea.

Keilly Michael

Second Grade

Keilly Michael graduated from Dallas Baptist University with a B.A. in Early Childhood Education. She is originally from Honduras, but moved to Texas with her family, at the age of 15, to finish her studies as well as pursue a college degree. After graduating college, she married and moved to Georgia where her husband ran his family business. Keilly has three years of teaching experience in the public and private school setting, many years of Sunday school and youth group teaching, and has served as a substitute teacher in the Cherokee County school district. Keilly and her husband Jon, have three boys; they enjoy playing board games, outdoor activities, playing sports such as soccer and beach volleyball and traveling. Keilly is passionate about teaching and hopes to share the love of Christ as she nurtures and guides her student's learning experience at Stonehaven.

Pam Grote

Third Grade

Pam Grote graduated from Wheaton College with a B.A. in Modern Foreign Languages and obtained her teaching degree from Kean University. She has taught in Christian schools in five states, beginning her career in NJ. After raising a family and homeschooling their four children, Pam returned to the classroom and has taught for over 25 years at different grade levels. Believing passionately in the classical Christian model of education, she loves teaching at Stonehaven where truth, beauty, and goodness from God's Word are incorporated into classroom studies daily. She is the Special Days Coordinator and enthusiastically wears Cambridge Gold and a funny wig whenever she can. Pam and Rich, her husband of 40 years, are active members at Christ The King in Kennesaw. They rejoice in God's sovereignty, and love to read good books, travel, and spend time with their nine grandchildren.

Maggie Bowling

Fourth Grade

An aspiring cat lady, Maggie Bowling graduated from Radford University with a degree in history. When she was invited to teach missionary kids in Russia for a season, law school plans were derailed, and the experience cemented her love for education. She decided to classically homeschool her own children for nine years, teaching at the Master’s Academy in Marietta and contributing to the community there. Outside of classical education, Maggie has also spent three years supporting special needs students in the public school system and is devoted to that community. She has traveled twice with her family to Honduras to share God’s care for orphans and would love to go back with her husband Alan and their four teenage-to-adult kids. Her family attends Highlands Church in Marietta, where she serves as the children’s pastor. Maggie also loves to read, run, travel, and do crossword puzzles.

James Mastrianna

Fifth & Sixth Grade - Humanities

Born and raised in sunny Southern California, James Mastrianna recently moved to Marietta from Santa Cruz, California. He has been blessed with a beautiful wife of ten years, and they have four wild and sweet children. He has spent fifteen years in education roles and ministerial positions as Youth Pastor and Intern program director in the local church. Throughout his tenure, he's had the privilege of educating, discipling, and caring for many families and students. He is a student in the Masters' program at Westminster Theological Seminary (East) and a life-long learner at heart and particularly interested in Historical studies, Theology, American church history, Philosophy and ALL things Sports, especially the Dodgers. His Classical Education philosophy stems from a focus on discipleship pertaining to dealing with the issues of the heart. His goal is that discipleship is more than modified behavior and through instruction, discipline, and admonishment, he aims to cultivate virtue and godliness amidst difficult issues.

Heather Martin

Fifth & Sixth Grade - Math & Science

Heather Martin graduated from The University of Montevallo with a B.A. in Social Work. She worked for Glenwood Inc. in Birmingham, AL as an Intensive In-home Counselor for children with special needs for 4 years. After moving to the Atlanta area, she and her husband Blake joined Church of the Apostles. They are heavily involved in the children’s ministry through teaching Sunday school, VBS, and in particular helping to facilitate the special needs program for the past 15 years. Heather has worked as a private teacher and tutor since 2006. She chose to Classically homeschool her own children for the last decade. Outside of training up their 3 children, Blake and Heather love traveling to Cuba, Ecuador, and Tanzania to share the gospel and to encourage the saints. In her spare time, Heather may be found sewing, crocheting, reading, gardening, or painting.

Logic & Rhetoric School

Chris Blackman

Bible

Chris Blackman was born in California and grew up in Las Vegas NV but spent his formative years in the Chattanooga TN area. After high school he attended Kennesaw State University where he earned a B.A. in History with a minor in International Affairs. During his time in college Jesus got a hold of Chris's life and he began to feel the Lord calling him towards vocational ministry. He attended Reformed Theological Seminary where he graduated with a masters of divinity degree. While attending RTS Chris was hired by Stonehaven to work as their facilities manager and after school teacher. He also spent the last four years working as an ESL teacher to adult immigrants for a non-profit in Atlanta and serving as a pastoral intern at his church. Chris, his wife Kelsey, and daughter Genesis love hiking along the Chattahoochee river, exploring the rich diversity of food on Buford highway, and entertaining people in their home. The most sanctifying part of Chris's day is the short period of time in the morning before he can greedily guzzle his first cup of pure, unadulterated by sin, black cup of coffee. Its all down hill from there...

Leah Colvin

Bible

Leah Colvin was born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin and has lived in New York, North Carolina and Louisiana though calls Georgia home. She graduated from the University of Georgia with a Bachelors in Child and Family Development and from New Orleans Baptist Seminary with a Masters of Divinity. She has served in multiple churches but now works full time as the Girls Student Ministry Coordinator in STS at Church of the Apostles in Atlanta. She loves her job, her students and church but also loves traveling as much as possible, spending time with her friends and family, running, sleeping and anything in or by the water. She is passionate about a good museum and will be the one who reads every sign in the exhibit.

Susan Corley

Mathematics

A native Texan, Susan grew up as the daughter of a high school and college math teacher. She grew up wondering why anyone of sound mind would want to teach math as a profession?! After graduating from Texas A&M University (Whoop!) with a degree in Finance, she began a career in commercial banking. Following a few years as a stay-at-home-mom, she returned to the working world at a crisis pregnancy center as bookkeeper, parenting teacher and school speaker. Shortly after enrolling their girls in a Classical Christian school outside of Fort Worth, TX, Susan was asked to prayerfully consider teaching mid-level math. Within a few weeks, she completely understood the desire to teach and is now passionate about the Classical Christian school model. It is a joy to share that math is not a neutral subject, and it all "works" because of our loving Creator. Susan has been married to her husband, Clay, for 19 years and they have three daughters that attend Stonehaven. They are members of The Church of the Apostles. She enjoys exercise, reading when she can, and taking walks with her family and crazy dog, Amos.

Kevin Cown

Literature & Science

Kevin Cown has been teaching for eleven years and took a brief break to work as a youth pastor at his home church, Trinity Anglican. After earning his Bachelors in Middle Grade Education with a focus in science and English from Kennesaw State University, he went back for his Master's in English Education. Within his first two years of teaching he also became gifted certified through the Fulton county school system. He loves being in the classroom for many reasons, but his favorite are developing relationships with his students and helping them continue their love of learning. Mr. Cown loves to be outside traveling to new places, hiking in the wilderness, or just being in nature with his wife and two kids, Judah (8) and Josie (6). You also have likely seen him riding bikes to and from Stonehaven with his family. When he needs to feel productive, he loves to spend time on building projects around the house, playing guitar and drums, and cooking for family and friends. Additionally, you may find him discussing literature and thematic events with whomever will give him the opportunity.

Hannah Hanbury

Spanish

Hannah Hanbury earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in International Relations and Spanish Language and Literature from Samford University and a Master of Arts degree in Spanish from the University of Louisville. She holds a graduate certificate in Latin American Studies with additional graduate coursework in theology. Missions work is close to Hannah’s heart, and she enjoys meeting and learning about people from different cultures. She has traveled to five continents and published writing and translation with Coalición por el evangelio (The Gospel Coalition Spanish) as well as an ethnography on Hispanics in London, England. Hannah is a fourth-generation teacher and has taught Spanish in secondary and post-secondary settings. She also regularly teaches English online to children in China. Hannah and her husband Aaron have two young girls: Ellen and Elisabeth.

Luke Hancock

Literature & Science

Luke Hancock graduated from Liberty University in 2021 with a B.A. in Philosophy and a minor in Creation Studies. In the field of philosophy, Luke has published an article on the relation of morality and the existence of God. Classically educated himself, Luke has a passion for the classical canon and wrote his senior Honors thesis on classical education, storytelling, and philosophy. He enjoys spending his summers working at camp. He once hiked 50 miles on the Appalachian Trail and loves camping and the outdoors. He enjoys sports, especially soccer and Ultimate Frisbee. His favorite book is Perelandra by C.S. Lewis and, if given the chance, he’ll talk your ear off if you ask him about medieval cosmology and how it relates to the thought of C.S. Lewis. His other interests include foosball, superhero movies, theatre, and board games.

Melissa Hobson

Logic & Rhetoric

Melissa Hobson earned her BA in Communication Studies from Mississippi State University in Starkville, MS and her MA in Communication and Culture from Indiana University in Bloomington, IN. Melissa and husband David have been married for 20 years and have lived in Atlanta for 17. Daughter Avery (12) and son Liam (10) love sixth grade and fifth grade at Stonehaven respectively. David and Melissa worship at Trinity Anglican Mission near their home on the Westside. When she isn't teaching logic, biblical worldview, or sixth grade humanities, you can find her downloading podcasts, streaming true crime docs, or playing HQ trivia on her smartphone where she's won $20 or more. Melissa proudly serves as Head of Cambridge House.

Kathryn Jackson

Science & Mathematics

Kathryn Jackson earned her BS in Biochemistry and also a BEd in Secondary Science Education from the University of Georgia. (Go DAWGS!) Kathryn has been teaching in some capacity most of her adult life. Whether in the classroom, in a women's Bible study, or at home with her family, she can't help but teach others about the wonders of God and His Word. She and her husband, Eric, have three children, Canon, Anne, and Kate, and they attend Cumberland Community Church. When she is not pouring over lesson plans, you can find her curled up with a good book.

Tyler Proffitt

Literature & History

Tyler Proffitt graduated with his BA in History from the University of Georgia and went to the University of Mississippi for graduate school where he studied politics in the American South and the role of religion in the civil rights movement. He is in his fourth year at Stonehaven and teaches history and literature. Outside of the classroom, Tyler enjoys reading, running alongside the Chattahoochee River and enjoying all that Atlanta has to offer with his friends. During the summer, you can usually find Tyler exploring the American West or working at a summer camp in Northeast Alabama.

Chuck Rickman

Practicum Teacher

Chuck Rickman graduated with a Bachelor of Industrial Engineering from Georgia Tech and a Bachelor of English Literature from Georgia State. He and Susan have been married for 20 years, currently live in Smyrna and have two children, Alex who is a 7th grader at Stonehaven and Katie who after finishing 8th grade at Stonehaven is now attending Pebblebrook High School in the Performing Arts program. The family attends Buckhead Church where Susan and Chuck lead a small group for newly married couples. Chuck enjoys reading, from classical to postmodern fiction, running, and "Do it yourself" projects. He is also a part time handyman and helps with the facilities management at Stonehaven.

Matt Stover

Latin

After getting bit by the language bug in high school, Matt studied 5 languages en route to a B.A. in Linguistics from the University of Georgia and an M.A. in Applied Linguistics from Biola University, with plans to translate the Bible with Wycliffe. When those plans changed, he stumbled into an 11-year career in real estate finance. During that time, Matt never lost his love of languages or his desire to help others grow in wisdom, knowledge and discipleship with Jesus. He is thrilled for the opportunity to put those to work as a teacher at Stonehaven. Matt and his wife Ashleigh live in Smyrna with their sons Burke (8) and Josiah (6), and enjoy serving at The Square Church. On weekends the Stovers can often be found looking for wildlife on a local trail or having their hearts broken by the Georgia Bulldogs. A few of Matt’s favorite things are good books, donuts, coffee, and playing just about any sport.

Cocurricular Teachers

Megan Johnson

Music

Megan Johnson graduated from Covenant College with a BA in music, minoring in education. For seven years after graduation she taught private piano lessons on Lookout Mountain, Georgia. Four years ago she and her family moved to Marietta. She is a nationally certified teacher of music through the Music Teachers National Association. Megan and her husband, Aaron, have four children. Austin is in 2nd grade and Charlie is in Kindergarten at Stonehaven. Little brothers Silas and Willard are future Stonehaven Knights. Megan is very involved in her church, Geneva OPC as a member, piano accompanist, and Sunday school teacher. Megan is excited to be a part of the Stonehaven family. "I want my students to see God's hand in the order and beauty of the voice He has given each one of His children." In her spare time Megan enjoys being outside and playing games with her family.

Stephanie Harvey

Nature Studies

Stephanie Harvey received her BA in Theatre from Birmingham-Southern College followed by a degree in Professional Make-Up Design from Delamar Academy in London, England. Working in England and then back home in Atlanta, Stephanie spent several years in the entertainment and fashion industry as a make-up artist. She is married to Stu and their three children attend Stonehaven. Stephanie's passion of training eyes and hearts to see God's beauty has naturally grown through her family's investment in classical, Christian education and now has been enriched by the opportunity to help develop the Nature Studies program at Stonehaven. She loves being with her family outdoors, listening to great music and discovering wonderful children's literature. Stephanie is a member at Smyrna Presbyterian Church.

Natalia Hicks

Art

Natalia Hicks came from Russia, where she graduated from St. Petersburg (formerly Leningrad) State University with a Master's Degree in History and Bachelor's in Education. For many years, Natalia worked in museums across St. Petersburg as a tour guide and curator. While working at the Hermitage, she met her husband, John, and moved to America. For 17 years, Natalia worked as an art teacher at Whitefield Academy, a private Christian school in Smyrna, GA. Her first goal is for students to recognize the beauty of this marvelous universe. God worked as the first artist when He was creating it. Her second goal is to discover and cherish the gift of creativity in everyone, for people are made in His image. Having recently retired, she missed the opportunity to interact with students and is very excited to be a part of Stonehaven School. In her free time, Natalia paints with oils in her studio, hikes with her daughter, Rachel, and her Bernese Mountain Dog, Max, and can't wait to start traveling again with her husband John.

Debbie Childree

Art

Debbie Childree received her BA in Studio Art from Furman University followed by graduate work in Art Education from Georgia State. Prior to teaching, Debbie worked as a freelance stained glass artist and taught community art classes at the Buckhead YMCA and Emory University. She also worked as a graphic designer and owned her own advertising and consulting business. Once her sons reached school age, she embarked on the second career of teaching. Debbie served for 19 years as a middle and upper school art teacher at Whitefield Academy. She attended multiple national and state art educators conferences where she taught workshops on encouraging creativity and also on fused glass. Debbie’s goal for her students is that they feel encouraged and inspired to discover and use their God-given creativity in the art classroom and in their lives. In her spare time Debbie loves to paint, sail, jog, hike, bike, and travel with her husband – enjoying God’s creation. Debbie is feeling very blessed to be part of Stonehaven!

Teacher Aide

Christy joined the Stonehaven community as a parent 4 years ago and is thrilled to have the opportunity to support the school, teachers, and students as a part-time aide this year. Front Office

After graduation, she worked in many facets of the banking industry while also supporting her husband's career as an Air Force officer.
